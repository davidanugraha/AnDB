To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee
Plot Summary: Set in the racially segregated South during the 1930s, the story follows young Scout Finch as her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout’s eyes, we see themes of justice, morality, and the loss of innocence unfold.

1984 by George Orwell
Plot Summary: In a dystopian society governed by a totalitarian regime, Winston Smith works to rewrite history for the oppressive Party but secretly dreams of rebellion. The novel explores themes of surveillance, propaganda, and individuality in the face of authoritarian control.

Gone Girl by Gillian Flynn
Plot Summary: When Amy Dunne disappears on her fifth wedding anniversary, her husband, Nick, becomes the prime suspect. The narrative alternates between Nick's perspective and Amy's diary, revealing shocking twists about their toxic marriage and Amy's meticulous manipulation.

The Night Circus by Erin Morgenstern
Plot Summary: A mysterious circus, open only at night, becomes the stage for a competition between two young magicians, Celia and Marco. Bound by a powerful spell, their rivalry is complicated by their growing love for each other in a story full of magic, intrigue, and romance.

The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald
Plot Summary: Set in the Roaring Twenties, the novel tells the story of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire who throws lavish parties in hopes of reuniting with his lost love, Daisy Buchanan. Narrated by Nick Carraway, the book explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream.

The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides
Plot Summary: Alicia Berenson, a famous painter, shoots her husband and stops speaking entirely. A psychotherapist, Theo Faber, becomes obsessed with uncovering the truth behind her silence, leading to a gripping and unexpected conclusion.

The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho
Plot Summary: Santiago, a shepherd boy, journeys from Spain to Egypt in search of a treasure he has seen in his dreams. Along the way, he learns profound life lessons about destiny, following one's heart, and the interconnectedness of all things.

Circe by Madeline Miller
Plot Summary: A reimagining of Greek mythology, the book focuses on Circe, the daughter of the sun god Helios. Banished to a deserted island for her witchcraft, Circe discovers her power while crossing paths with iconic mythological figures like Odysseus and Hermes.

Dune by Frank Herbert
Plot Summary: Paul Atreides, heir to the noble House Atreides, relocates with his family to the desert planet Arrakis, home to the valuable spice melange. Betrayal, political intrigue, and prophecy shape Paul’s journey to becoming a messianic figure for the oppressed desert people.

Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens
Plot Summary: Abandoned as a child, Kya Clark grows up isolated in the marshlands of North Carolina. When a local man is found dead, Kya becomes a murder suspect. The novel weaves a mystery with themes of loneliness, survival, and the natural world.
